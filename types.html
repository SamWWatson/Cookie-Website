Detailed Cookie Types

Session Cookies

Temporary cookies that are only active during a single browsing session. They are deleted automatically once the browser is closed.

  • Keeping a user logged in during a session
  • Storing temporary form input to avoid loss on accidental page refresh

Persistent Cookies

Cookies that are stored on the user's device for a defined period.

  • "Remember me" logins or auto-login
  • Saving user preferences like languages and themes

First-Party Cookies

Cookies set by the website the user is directly visiting.

  • User authentication
  • Saving site preferences and personalization

Third-Party Cookies

Cookies created by external domains that are embedded on the visited website via ads or plugins.

  • Cross-site user tracking
  • Targeted advertising and retargeting

Secure Cookies

Cookies marked with the Secure attribute, meaning they are only transmitted over HTTPS connections.

  • Protecting session data from man-in-the-middle attacks
  • Helps prevent interception of cookie data during transmission

HTTP-Only Cookies

Cookies marked with the HttpOnly flag are inaccessible to JavaScript and only used in HTTP requests.

  • Storing session tokens and sensitive identifiers
  • Often paired with Secure for safer cookies
